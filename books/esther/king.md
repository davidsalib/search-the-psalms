[**Esther 9:12**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — And the **king** said to Queen Esther, “The Jews have killed and destroyed five hundred men in Shushan the citadel, and the ten sons of Haman. What have they done in the rest of the **king**’s provinces? Now what is your petition? It shall be granted to you. Or what is your further request? It shall be done.”

[**Esther 9:13**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — Then Esther said, “If it pleases the **king**, let it be granted to the Jews who are in Shushan to do again tomorrow according to today’s decree, and let Haman’s ten sons be hanged on the gallows.”

[**Esther 9:14**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — So the **king** commanded this to be done; the decree was issued in Shushan, and they hanged Haman’s ten sons.

[**Esther 9:16**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — The remainder of the Jews in the **king**’s provinces gathered together and protected their lives, had rest from their enemies, and killed seventy-five thousand of their enemies; but they did not lay a hand on the plunder.

[**Esther 9:20**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — And Mordecai wrote these things and sent letters to all the Jews, near and far, who were in all the provinces of **King** Ahasuerus,

[**Esther 9:25**](../bible/esther/esther-9.md) — but when Esther came before the **king**, he commanded by letter that this wicked plot which Haman had devised against the Jews should return on his own head, and that he and his sons should be hanged on the gallows.

[**Esther 10:1**](../bible/esther/esther-10.md) — And **King** Ahasuerus imposed tribute on the land and on the islands of the sea.

[**Esther 10:2**](../bible/esther/esther-10.md) — Now all the acts of his power and his might, and the account of the greatness of Mordecai, to which the **king** advanced him, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the **kings** of Media and Persia?

[**Esther 10:3**](../bible/esther/esther-10.md) — For Mordecai the Jew was second to **King** Ahasuerus, and was great among the Jews and well received by the multitude of his brethren, seeking the good of his people and speaking peace to all his countrymen.
